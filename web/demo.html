How It Works

RRann generates random numbers by computing the same operation (seed^seed) at two different precisions simultaneously:

seed (float) → compute at IEEE 754 (53-bit) AND Decimal (80-digit)
↓
Find divergence point (where digits differ due to rounding)
↓
Extract digits from high-precision representation
↓
Normalize to [0, 1) and mix with seed
↓
Output: random float

The entropy comes from the rounding error itself—the bits that IEEE 754 cannot represent are mathematically unpredictable and fully random.
